Mission Launch - 1953

Space Foods Company was an American business located in Baltimore, Maryland. An idea before it's time, the company developed a line called Galaxy Home Syrups...for Soft Drinks. The homemade soda kit collection consisted of 10 different ranks of the crew members with 8 different colors. The bottles also had a screw-on cap with flavor label. The five flavors were Cherry, Raspberry, Lemon-lime, Grape and Orange. The caps also include a slot that allowed the bottles to be used as a coin bank to hold what we now call "old dumb money".

These awesome bottles were put into production and boxed in cases of 12. We do not know when the company planned to launch these kits to the public, but in 1953 Space Foods Company filed for bankruptcy and deemed the initial launch a failure.

Almost 20 years later in 1971, our Space Ace's grandfather purchased a building for his manufacturing company. To his surprise, the building came with more than just factory floor space. The upstairs was home to thousands of these boxed crew members, who never had the chance to complete their launch. Space Ace's family has sold a few to collectors, antique stores and even a local restaurant but never found a way to tell the story of these bottles. Until now. Now, Space Foods Company has the opportunity to tell the story of these unique antique bottles. A story that we will tell together as a community through our art inspired by the physical bottles themselves! Join us in our new mission to share a piece of untold history with the world.
